CREAMY PUMPKIN SOUP

Ingredients

1/4 cup Land O Lakes® Butter

1 small (1/4 cup) onion, chopped

1 clove garlic, finely chopped

2 teaspoons firmly packed brown sugar

1 (14.5-ounce) can chicken stock or broth

1/2 cup water

1/2 teaspoon salt

1/4 teaspoon pepper

1 (15-ounce) can 100% Pure Pumpkin

1 (12-ounce) can evaporated milk

1 cup heavy cream

1/8 teaspoon ground cinnamon

Several pinches of fresh ground nutmeg (must be fresh)

Instructions

1. Melt butter in 3-quart saucepan until sizzling. Add onion, garlic and sugar; cook over medium heat until onion is softened (1 to 2 minutes).

2. Add stock, water, salt and pepper; bring to a boil, stirring occasionally. Reduce heat to low; cook, stirring occasionally, for 15 minutes. Stir in pumpkin, evaporated milk and cinnamon. Cook, stirring occasionally, for 5 minutes. Remove from heat.

3. Transfer mixture to food processor bowl fitted with metal blade or 5-cup blender container (in batches, if necessary). Cover; process until smooth. Return mixture to saucepan. Serve warm.

GRILLED STRAWBERRY COBBLER WITH ICE CREAM

Ingredients

6 wooden or metal skewer

2 pounds fresh strawberries, stems removed

1 cup strawberry preserves (Truffles and Trifles)

¼ cup water

2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice

½ cup butter (one stick)

2 cups Bisquick or other all purpose baking mix

2 cups of sugar or non-sugar substitute

1-1/2 cups half and half

EQUIPMENT NEEDED:

13 x 9-inch glass casserole dish

Huller

Cutting board

Bread knife

Measuring cups - wet and dry

dish for soaking skewers

Vegetable spray for grill

Small saucepan

Medium bowl

Flat whisk

Ice Cream recipe follows

1. Soak wooden skewers for 1 hour or use metal ones. Spray grill rack with nonstick, nonflammable cooking spray. Preheat grill to high heat – 400-450 degrees.

2. Thread whole strawberries onto skewers. Arrange skewers on grill and grill for 1 minute on each side. Remove from grill and slice strawberries and set aside.

3. In a small saucepan, combine strawberry preserves, water and lemon juice over medium heat. Bring to a simmer; cook for 2 minutes.

4. Preheat oven to 350 degrees.

5. Place butter into a 13 x 9-inch glass baking dish. Place dish in oven and melt butter.

6. In a medium bowl, combine baking mix, sugar and half and half. Pour batter carefully into dish on top of melted butter. Arrange sliced strawberries over batter. Pour preserve mixture over strawberries.

7. Bake for 50 to 55 minutes or until golden brown all over surface with no white showing and is set. Serve warm with ice cream.

BEEF STROGANOFF 1 small yellow onion 1 tablespoon butter 1 tablespoon minced garlic, smashed with a meat pounder 1 pound lean ground beef, 7 % fat 4 ounces fresh baby portabellas or white mushrooms ¼ teaspoon salt 1/8 teaspoon pepper ¼ cup flour 8 ounces wide egg noodles 10.5 ounce can of beef stock or broth 8 ounces of sour cream 1. Put water on to boil for pasta 2. Peel and dice onion, leaving on root end. 3. Melt butter in large skillet on medium-high heat, and then add onions and garlic; cook and stir for 1 minute to soften. Add beef, mushrooms, salt and pepper (wash hands); cook and stir occasionally for 5 to 7 minutes or until meat crumbles and no pink remains. 4. Stir flour into beef mixture; cook and stir for 2 minutes. 5. Cook pasta following package instructions. 6. Slowly stir in broth into meat mixture; and then stir in sour cream using a flat whisk. Reduce heat to medium; cook and stir 7-8 minutes to blend flavors. 7. Drain pasta; serve beef mixture over noodles.
CHEESE CHICKEN TORTILLA SOUP 1 medium yellow onion, chopped 2 teaspoons fresh garlic, minced 2 teaspoons vegetable oil 5 cups chicken stock ¼ cup chopped bell pepper of any color 15 ounces can tomato puree 1 teaspoon finely minced jalapeno pepper ½ teaspoon salt or to taste ¼ or more freshly ground pepper or to taste 1 teaspoon sugar 1 teaspoon chili powder 1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce ½ teaspoon hot sauce 4 teaspoons flour ½ cup water 1 pound cooked chicken, cubed or pulled (Deli chicken is good choice) 1 cup cream ¼ cup sour cream 8 ounces medium Cheddar cheese – cut in 1-inch cubes 10 tortillas cut into ¼-inch strips and fried Fresh cilantro (optional), roughly chopped Sea salt for tortillas Sauté onions with oil in large pan or Dutch oven until soft, add garlic. Cook for 2 additional minutes. Add all ingredients up through hot sauce. Bring to a low boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 20 minutes. Cut tortillas into ¼-inch strips and fry in deep fryer. Sprinkle with sea salt. Thoroughly mix flour and water and then whisk into soup. Bring to a low boil, and simmer for 5 minutes. Stir in sour cream and cheese. When serving soup, pile tortilla strips on top of soup. Garnish with cilantro.
