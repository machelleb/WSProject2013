HOPPIN’ JOHN SALAD

I always serve Hoppin’ John on New Year’s Eve. Here is a twist with a cold salad that can be made the day before.

  • 2 cups dried black-eyed peas, picked over and rinsed
  • 1 medium yellow onion, coarsely chopped
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • ½ cup diced smoked ham
  • 4 scallions, white and green parts, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • ½ cup olive oil
  • ½ cup Wesson vegetable oil
  • ½ cup red wine vinegar
  • 2 large garlic cloves, smashed with meat pounder and then chopped
  • ¼ teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 cup popcorn rice, cooked (recipe included)
  1. 1. Place the black-eyed peas in large Dutch oven with water to cover. Cook over medium-high heat and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 2 minutes. Remove from heat, cover and let stand 1 hour.
  2. 2. Drain peas, rinse and return to pot. Cover with fresh water and add the chopped onion, bay leaves and salt. Return to stove and bring mixture to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer peas for 1 hour, or until tender BUT not mushy. Drain peas, remove bay leaves and cool.
  3. 3. Place peas, ham, scallions and red bell pepper in a large attractive bowl.
  4. 4. To make dressing – I put oils, vinegar, garlic and cayenne pepper in a blender and blend completely for 1 to 2 minutes. Pour dressing over pea mixture and toss well to mix all ingredients. Cover bowl with plastic wrap and put in refrigerator to marinate overnight.
  5. 5. Cook rice and refrigerate in covered bowl.
  6. 6. A few hours before serving, remove pea salad from refrigerator along with rice. Add rice to salad and mix well and chill until serving time.

    7. EQUIPMENT NEEDED: Dutch oven Cutting board Sharp knife Meat pounder for garlic Measuring cups Measuring spoons Colander to drain peas Large attractive serving bowl Slotted spoon Blender plastic wrap Skillet with lid for cooking rice
